Stone age technology — but magical. Materials: wood, stone, bone, clay, leather, hide, fur, plant fiber, bark, reeds, resin, tar, sinew, gut, horn, antler, teeth, feathers, shells, fossils, chitin, crystals, charcoal, ash, pigments, fungi, and other natural remains. Metal is near-mythical and treated as magical when found.

Animals are hybrids of two real animals, named in Swedish combination (e.g. näbbfink = näbbmus + fink).

Plants are described in three parts: appearance, nature, and genus (e.g. Vitblek myrkantarell, Massivt lysfrö, Vaksam månfura).

The seven major religions:
Mitriki — knowledge, memory, writing, wisdom, truth, heritage
Folgor — family, protection, instinct, blood, courage, cohesion
Sternine — freedom, independence, defiance, curiosity, self-determination
Ebba — sea, movement, change, flow, depth, yielding
Kung Kallus — earth, fertility, weight, reproduction, tribe, survival
Viaträdet — life, connection, balance, roots, web, cycle
Mortmori — death, rebirth, impermanence, rest, decay, inevitability

Advenire: a higher dimension, path of the soul after death, dwelling place of gods and their emissaries, a plane where emotions take physical form. No one knows what it truly is — only that its power permeates Viaproxima. Mages open themselves toward it to channel magic.

HV (Handlingsvärde) = action value. Expressed as +1 HV on specific actions or sub-stats. ~1 HV = 3 points on a 1–20 stat scale.
CV = casting value (Kristallsejdare). KV = ritual power value (Shaman).

Religion does not belong to items related to kristallsejdar items and shaman items.
